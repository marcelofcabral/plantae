Plantae

Get to know more about some common house plants

Lavender

Lavandula (common name lavender) is a genus of 47 known species of flowering plants in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the Old World and is found in Cape Verde and the Canary Islands, and from Europe across to northern and eastern Africa, the Mediterranean, southwest Asia to India.

Growing tips

Lavenders flourish best in dry, well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils in full sun.

All types need little or no fertilizer and good air circulation.

In areas of high humidity, root rot due to fungus infection can be a problem.

Rose

A rose is a woody perennial flowering plant of the genus Rosa, in the family Rosaceae, or the flower it bears.

There are over three hundred species and tens of thousands of cultivars. They form a group of plants that can be erect shrubs, climbing, or trailing, with stems that are often armed with sharp prickles.

Growing tips

Roses crave sun, at least six hours a day is ideal.

Plant roses in rich, well-draining soil. When planting, mix organic matter, such as compost or ground bark, into excavated soil you will use to backfill the planting hole.

Irrigate roses deeply but infrequently, applying water directly to soil using soaker hoses or drip irrigation. Water needs vary based on weather and soil, so check soil with your finger.

Spearmint

Spearmint, also known as garden mint, is a species of mint, Mentha spicata, native to Europe and southern temperate Asia, extending from Ireland in the west to southern China in the east.

It is used as a flavouring in food and herbal teas. The aromatic oil, called oil of spearmint, is also used as a flavouring and sometimes as a scent.

Growing tips

Divide plants every two years to keep them healthy.

If you live in an area with very cold winters, it is best to bring potted spearmint indoors and place in a sunny window.

Although it can grow in many different types of soil, it always needs plenty of water. During summer, ensure the soil is moist to the touch at all times.
